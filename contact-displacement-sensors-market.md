<p><p>Contact displacement sensors are used to measure the position or movement of an object through physical contact. In the market, two primary types are identified: Analog and Digital. Analog contact displacement sensors provide a continuous output signal proportional to the displacement, allowing for precise measurements. In contrast, Digital contact displacement sensors deliver discrete signals, often providing precise position readings in specific increments. Both types have applications across manufacturing, automation, and instrumentation, catering to varying needs for accuracy and data interpretation.</p></p>
