<p><p>The fermented citric acid market primarily consists of two types: Citric Acid Anhydrous (CAA) and Citric Acid Monohydrate (CAM). CAA is a dry, crystalline form with no water content, making it suitable for applications requiring high purity and stability. In contrast, CAM contains one molecule of water, offering advantages in solubility and is commonly used in food, beverages, and pharmaceuticals. Both forms cater to diverse industries, emphasizing the versatility and essential role of citric acid in various applications.</p></p>
