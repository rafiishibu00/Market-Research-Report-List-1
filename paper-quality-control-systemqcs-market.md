<p><p>Paper Quality Control Systems (QCS) are essential in the pulp and paper industry for monitoring and maintaining quality during production. The market is segmented into two main types: Machine Direction (MD) and Cross-machine Direction (CD). MD focuses on quality measurements along the direction of the paper flow, ensuring uniform properties such as thickness and moisture. In contrast, CD concentrates on the quality across the width of the paper, addressing variations that can affect overall performance and appearance. Both directions are crucial for optimal quality assurance.</p></p>
