<p><p>Through beam sensors are a type of optical sensor consisting of a transmitter and a receiver positioned directly opposite each other. They work by emitting a beam of light (typically infrared) that, when interrupted by an object, triggers an output signal. <p><p>Through beam sensors are critical in various applications, utilizing two main types: infrared and laser sensors. Infrared type sensors emit infrared light from a transmitter to a receiver, detecting obstacles by measuring interruptions in the beam. They are commonly used for object detection and safety applications. In contrast, laser type sensors use a focused laser beam, offering greater accuracy and longer detection ranges. This precision makes them ideal for industrial automation and complex environments where enhanced performance is required.</p></p>
