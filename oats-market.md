<p><p>Oats are categorized into three main market types: Groats, Regular/Flakes, and Powder/Flour. Groats are the whole, hulled grains, offering the most nutrients and often used in cooked dishes. Regular oats, or flakes, are steamed and rolled, providing a quick-cooking option for breakfast items. Powder or flour is made by grinding oats into a fine texture, commonly used in baking, smoothies, and gluten-free recipes. Each type caters to different consumer preferences and culinary applications.</p></p>
